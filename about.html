Who We Are

Turning waste into wealth, one community at a time.

Our Story

Zuka Yethu addresses critical socio-economic challenges—unemployment, food insecurity, and environmental pollution—through innovation rooted in indigenous knowledge and vermi-composting science.

Our flagship product, Zuka Gud Grow, is a two-in-one liquid organic compost and natural pest repellent, made from fruit, vegetable, and garden waste. It boosts crop yields, improves soil health, and eliminates the need for harmful chemicals.

“Zuka” means the 5c coin—a symbol of overlooked value. Just as people discard these coins, communities often discard organic waste. We help them see the hidden worth in what others call trash.

About Our Founder

Sizwe Mnamatha was born in KwaNoBuhle, Uitenhage (Eastern Cape). Inspired by his late grandmother Nqabisa Habana—who taught him to live off the land in Mhlangamisweni—he developed a deep respect for sustainable living.

Introduced to vermi-composting at Rhodes University in 2009, Sizwe combined this knowledge with a vision for social enterprise. He left a corporate career at Telkom to tackle poverty, food insecurity, and waste pollution head-on.

His work has been recognized through awards including the GAP Green Innovation Competition (3rd Place, 2014), Pitch + Polish (SA FM), and support from agricultural departments in the Eastern and Western Cape.

Our Mission & Objectives

Core Mission

To empower communities to unlock the value in resources deemed worthless—especially organic waste—and transform them into tools for food security, employment, and environmental healing.

Strategic Goals
  • Establish small social enterprises nationwide
  • Train volunteers in organic farming and nursery management
  • Create community seed banks from waste
  • Eliminate food insecurity through sustainable agriculture
  • Reduce pollution via affordable waste-to-wealth systems
