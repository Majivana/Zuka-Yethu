Community Outreach

Changing mindsets, creating wealth from waste

“Zuka means the 5c coin—overlooked, discarded, yet still valuable. We help communities see worth in what others call waste.”

Our outreach empowers people to shift from dependency to dignity through enterprise, education, and environmental action.

Our Outreach Initiatives

Little Flower & Marrymount Job Fund

What began as a school trip fundraiser evolved into a community enterprise. Students and families collect izuka (5c coins) to fund garden tools, car-washing kits, and job creation near Little Flower Primary School.

The project integrates real-world learning in business, science, and environmental stewardship while creating jobs for unemployed neighbors.

MJITA Brand

We mentor Pumza Fibi of Motherwell, Port Elizabeth, in sustainable clothing manufacturing. Starting with glue (and learning from early setbacks), she now sews practical, market-ready items.

Under the MJITA label, she creates boxer shorts, scarves, bucket hats, and neck pillows for commuters. Her 2015 debut at the National Arts Festival in Grahamstown led to sales and orders from Cape Town to Pretoria.

Project Playground Partnership

We support Project Playground—an NGO serving children who use wheelchairs—in Langa, Cape Town, led by Hazel Gocina.

Zuka mobilized students from TSiBA Education and LEAP Science & Maths School to collect bread bag tags. These were exchanged with a recycling partner for a much-needed wheelchair—turning small waste into life-changing mobility.
